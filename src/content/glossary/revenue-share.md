# What is Revenue Share

**Revenue share is a business model where a company or platform distributes a predetermined percentage of the income it generates from a user, creator, or partner's activity back to that individual or entity.**

In the context of platforms like OnlyFans, revenue share specifically refers to the split of subscription fees, tips, and pay-per-view (PPV) content sales between the platform itself and the content creator. For example, a common revenue share model is 80/20, meaning the creator keeps 80% of the income they generate, while the platform retains 20% as a fee for hosting, providing payment processing, and delivering the tools and infrastructure. This model directly aligns the platform's success with the creator's success; as a creator earns more, so does the platform.

This differs from other monetization structures like flat fees or salaries. Revenue share is inherently performance-based and scalable. A creator's earnings are not capped by an hourly rate but are directly tied to their ability to build and engage a paying subscriber base. The platform's cut covers operational costs (servers, bandwidth, customer support) and profit, while the creator's majority share compensates them for content creation, marketing, and community management.

Understanding revenue share is critical for creators when choosing a platform, as it directly impacts their potential income and business sustainability. A platform with a higher creator share (e.g., 80/20) is generally more attractive, but creators must also evaluate the value of the platform's tools, audience reach, and support services. For affiliate marketers or tool providers listed on directories like BestOnlyFans, revenue share can also refer to commission programs where they earn a percentage of sales referred to a platform or service.
