# What is Tip Menu

**A Tip Menu is a curated, pre-set list of monetary amounts or specific services that a content creator provides to their audience, facilitating and encouraging custom tips or payments.**

The Tip Menu functions as a digital price list or interactive catalog, typically posted prominently on a creator's profile or sent directly to fans. Instead of a fan guessing an appropriate tip amount or negotiating a custom request, they can choose from clear, standardized options. These menus often list specific dollar amounts (e.g., $5, $10, $25) alongside corresponding general thank-you rewards, or they can be more granular, linking specific amounts to particular types of content or actions. For example, a menu might state "$10 for a personalized thank-you video" or "$25 for a custom set of photos." This structure streamlines the transaction, making it a simple click-and-pay process for the supporter.

From a creator's perspective, a Tip Menu is a crucial tool for monetizing engagement beyond subscription walls. It provides a direct path for fans to show extra appreciation, fund specific goals, or commission personalized content without the need for lengthy back-and-forth messages. It effectively up-sells the audience by presenting clear, tempting options, thereby increasing average revenue per fan. For fans, it demystifies the tipping process, sets clear expectations on what their money will "get them," and reduces the social awkwardness of deciding an amount, making them more likely to engage.

**Why it matters / Use cases:** Tip Menus are fundamental for creator revenue diversification. They are used to unlock higher levels of interaction, fund special projects (like buying new equipment), receive support for public posts, or commission custom content such as dedicated shout-outs, specific photo sets, or the fulfillment of fan-named requests. A well-designed menu educates new fans on how to engage further and serves as a constant, low-effort call-to-action for existing subscribers.
