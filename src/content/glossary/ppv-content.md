# What is PPV Content

**PPV (Pay-Per-View) Content is a monetization model where creators charge a one-time fee for access to a specific piece of content, such as a video, photo set, or message, rather than including it in a standard subscription.**

In the context of platforms like OnlyFans, PPV content is typically sent directly to subscribers via private message or made available in a locked post on the creator's feed. While a fan may pay a monthly subscription fee to access a creator's general feed, PPV content is an additional, optional purchase. This creates a tiered access system: the subscription often provides regular content, while PPV offers exclusive, premium, or more explicit material. The key mechanic is that the content is not viewable until the specific fee is paid, protecting the creator's revenue and allowing them to market directly to their most engaged audience.

This model is central to many creators' earnings strategies. It allows for flexible pricing—a short clip might cost a few dollars, while a full-length, high-production video could be priced at $50 or more. Common examples include exclusive behind-the-scenes footage, full-length videos (e.g., "My Beach Vacation Vlog"), custom content commissioned by a fan, special themed photo sets (e.g., a holiday series), or archived content from earlier in a creator's career. Creators often use their general feed to preview or tease PPV content, such as posting a thumbnail or short trailer to generate interest.

PPV matters because it maximizes a creator's income potential. It allows them to earn from both their subscriber base (via the monthly fee) and their super-fans willing to pay extra for exclusivity. For fans, it provides a way to support creators more directly for specific content they truly want, often with a higher degree of privacy for the transaction as it occurs in a direct message. For platforms, it drives higher transaction volumes and user engagement.
